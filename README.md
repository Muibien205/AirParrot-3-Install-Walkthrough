## 🎯 Troubleshooting common errors

Installation might fail for several reasons. Use these solutions if you encounter issues.

**Setup Failed Error**
This error often occurs when previous versions of the software remain on your drive. Uninstall any old instances of AirParrot through the Windows Control Panel. Restart your computer after you remove the old version. Then, run the new installer again.

**Driver Installation Issues**
AirParrot needs specific drivers to capture your screen. If the install fails during the driver phase, restart your PC in Safe Mode and clear your temporary files folder. Remove existing drivers via Device Manager before you try the installation process again.

**Antivirus Constraints**
Some security programs flag screen mirroring tools. Disable your third-party antivirus for ten minutes while you install the software. Turn the protection back on once the installation finishes. Add AirParrot to the exclusion list of your security suite to prevent future conflicts.

**Network Discovery**
If you cannot find your mirroring target, ensure your network profile is set to Private. Open Settings, go to Network & internet, and select Properties for your connection. Choose Private instead of Public. This allows Windows to discover other devices on your local network.

## 🟢 Getting started

Once the installation ends, launch AirParrot 3 from your Start menu. The app icon appears in your system tray, usually near the clock at the bottom right corner of the screen.

Click the icon to open the main menu. The app searches for available devices on your local network. Select your destination device from the list. Choose the specific screen you want to mirror or share. You can also share specific applications instead of your entire desktop.

Adjust the quality settings in the Preferences menu if the mirror feels slow. Lowering the resolution or frame rate helps keep the connection steady on older hardware.

## 💻 Technical support

If problems persist, check your network hardware. A weak Wi-Fi signal causes lag and disconnection. Connect your computer to the router using a wired Ethernet connection to test if the issue relates to signal strength. Ensure both your computer and your receiving device operate on the same subnet.
